# NBA-Player-Performance-Analysis
Tableau analysis of NBA player performance and scoring efficiency during the 2021–2022 season.
NBA Player Performance Analysis (Tableau)
Project Overview

This project explores NBA player performance during the 2021–2022 season using interactive visualizations built in Tableau.

The goal of the analysis is to identify patterns in scoring behavior, playmaking, shooting efficiency, and player roles across different positions and age groups.

By visualizing player statistics across teams and positions, the dashboard highlights how different types of players contribute to team performance.

Tools

Tableau – data visualization and dashboard development

Excel / CSV dataset – NBA player statistics

GitHub – project documentation and portfolio presentation

Key Analytical Questions

This analysis focuses on several questions about player performance:

Which players contribute the most points across the league?

Do players who score more points also produce more assists?

Which positions are most efficient at 3-point shooting?

How does scoring distribution vary by player age group?

Which positions dominate assist creation?

Dashboard Visualizations
Player Performance: Points vs Assists

This scatter plot explores the relationship between scoring and playmaking.

Key observations:

Players like Trae Young, Chris Paul, and Dejounte Murray stand out as elite playmakers.

Some high scorers (e.g., Joel Embiid, Jayson Tatum) focus more on scoring than assists.

Guards dominate high-assist regions of the chart.

3-Point Shooting Efficiency by Position

This heatmap shows how three-point efficiency varies by position and team.

Insights:

Guards generally show stronger three-point shooting performance.

Centers display larger variation, depending on team strategy.

Some teams rely heavily on perimeter shooting from specific positions.

Assist Leaders by Position

This visualization highlights which players generate the most assists.

Insights:

Point Guards dominate assist creation, reflecting their role as primary playmakers.

Players like Trae Young and Chris Paul lead assist totals.

Other positions contribute less frequently but can still impact team offense.

Points by Age Group

This chart analyzes how scoring varies across player age groups.

Insights:

The 25–29 age group produces the highest scoring output, representing the peak performance years.

Younger players contribute less overall but show rising potential.

Scoring output gradually declines in later career stages.

Points Distribution by Player

This visualization shows how scoring is distributed among players.

Insights:

Most teams rely heavily on one primary scoring star.

Examples include:

Nikola Jokić (DEN)

Joel Embiid (PHI)

Trae Young (ATL)

Luka Dončić (DAL)

Key Insights
Star-Driven Scoring

Many NBA teams rely on a single dominant scorer who produces a large share of total points.

Scoring vs Playmaking Tradeoff

Players who lead in scoring are not always the top assist producers. Playmaking is concentrated among guards.

Positional Shooting Patterns

Guards generally demonstrate higher three-point efficiency, while centers show greater variability.

Peak Performance Age

The 25–29 age group represents the peak scoring years for NBA players.

Playmaking Roles

Point guards dominate assist production, confirming their role as primary offensive facilitators.
